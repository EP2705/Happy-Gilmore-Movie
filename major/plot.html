Plot
 

Happy Gilmore is a short-tempered, unsuccessful ice hockey player whose only skills are fighting and a strong slapshot that he learnt from his deceased father. His inability to skate limits his career opportunities. When he returns home from yet another failed tryout, his girlfriend, Terry, a kindergarten teacher, rejects him due to his lack of success.

 

The next day, Happy discovers that his grandmother owes the IRS $270,000 in past taxes; she has 90 days to repay the amount or face eviction from her home. Happy sends his grandma to a retirement home, where residents are secretly mistreated, until he can figure out how to repay the bill.

Happy joins a pro tour in hopes of winning lots of money to buy his grandma's house. He becomes a fan favourite on the tour, thanks to his exceptionally long drives and unconventional behaviour. He also meets arrogant pro golfer Shooter McGavin, the tour's dominant player, who disapproves of Happy's poor golf etiquette. 

Happy is now determined to win the Tour Championship. On the 18th hole, a television tower damaged by a car falls onto the green and blocks Happy's line to the hole, but Happy miraculously uses it as a Rube Goldberg machine to sink his putt for the win and is able to buy his grandma's house back.
